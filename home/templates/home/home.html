What does OCEAN mean? What does it stand for?

Despite the 7 billion (and counting) different personalities, psychologists have narrowed them down to five important ones, commonly called the OCEAN model of personality. Any individual's personality traits can be approximated using this model. Interestingly, people who act similarly (best friends, significant others, siblings) also happen to be quite similar on this model's scale. Take the test today to find out how similar you are to your closest and dearest.

OCEAN stands for

Openness

This measures how open you are when interacting with others. Do you share your feelings with others, or do you prefer to keep them to yourself? The less open you are, the less you score on this scale.

Learn More

Conscientiousness

Are you neat? Do you prefer a definite schedule in your life? Or perhaps you get yelled at by your fellow society members that your room is dirtier than Harry's Cupboard Under the Stairs. If so, you're definitely low on this scale.

Learn More

Extraversion

Pretty self explanatory. If the thought of going to a gathering and socializing makes you jump in glee, you're not lacking any extraversion.

Learn More

Agreeableness

Do people like your company? Are you able to get people to do something you want easily? Then you score pretty good here.

Learn More

Neuroticism

This trait deals mostly with the emotional part of your personality. People who are high in neuroticism are generally more anxious, prone to depression and lose their temper easily. People on the opposite side of this scale are laid back and have a positive outlook towards life.
